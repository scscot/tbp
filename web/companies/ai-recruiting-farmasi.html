What Farmasi Sells (in plain English)

Farmasi is a Turkish-based beauty and personal care company offering cosmetics, skincare, fragrance, and home care products through a catalog model. The flagship lines include Make-up color cosmetics (foundations, lipsticks, eyeshadows with extensive shade ranges), Dr. C. Tuna skincare (dermatologically tested formulations), fragrances (designer-inspired scents at accessible prices), and Tunaskin professional skin care. Additional categories include body care, hair care, men's grooming, baby care, and home cleaning products. Founded in 1950, Farmasi emphasizes Turkish beauty heritage, dermatologist-developed formulas, and affordable luxury positioning.

Key positioning: Catalog beauty with shade variety—think Avon meets Sephora at direct-selling prices.

Markets & Footprint

Farmasi operates globally with presence in North America (USA, Canada), Europe, Latin America, and expanding internationally. The company leverages digital catalogs, social selling tactics, and beauty party models. Farmasi is particularly strong among beauty influencers, makeup enthusiasts, and entrepreneurs seeking flexible income streams through catalog sales.

Catalog-driven model means your recruiting must work through visual demonstrations and shade-matching.

Distribution Model

Farmasi uses a unilevel compensation plan with fast-start bonuses and team overrides. Beauty Influencers earn through retail sales commissions (typically 20-25% margins), team volume bonuses, and rank advancement incentives. The company emphasizes party-based selling (virtual or in-person), sampling strategies, and social media content creation. Monthly personal sales requirements are minimal, focusing instead on customer acquisition.

Key insight: Shade matching beats product lists. Start with foundation/lipstick matching, expand from there.

Training & Onboarding Snapshot

Farmasi emphasizes catalog party training with sampling kits, virtual event hosting, and social media content calendars. New Beauty Influencers are taught shade-matching techniques (ask for selfie in natural light), bundle by look strategies (work/weekend/glam), and simple reorder workflows. Corporate training includes catalog navigation, compliance basics (avoid medical claims), and team duplication systems. The challenge: converting party attendees into consistent customers requires automated follow-up and sample tracking.

Your AI Recruiting Playbook for Farmasi

  1. Lead with shade matching. Ask for a selfie in natural window light. AI analyzes and suggests foundation/lipstick shades from catalog. This beats generic invites.
  2. 15-minute mini lives. Short product demos (one look, 3-4 products) work better than hour-long catalog overviews. AI scripts these quick demos.
  3. Bundle by look automation. Work look (neutral palette + long-wear foundation), weekend casual (tinted moisturizer + lip stain), glam night (smokey eye + bold lip). AI builds carts.
  4. Sampling rules system. Match shade before mailing samples. AI tracks who got what, when to follow up, and what questions to ask.
  5. Weekly party + Q&A rhythm. One demo, one Q&A session per week. AI generates invites, reminders, and post-event cart links.
  6. Referral ask timing. After first purchase success, ask "Who are two friends who love makeup?" AI scripts the exact referral message.
  7. Duplicate the party pack. Save your demo scripts, look bundles, and follow-up sequences so new Influencers can host in 48 hours.
