What 3 International Sells (in plain English)

Getting Started with 3 International: Your First 30 Days

New to 3 International? Here's your step-by-step roadmap to launching your distributor journey with health supplements and wellness education:

  1. Day 1-3: Foundation & Personal Health Assessment — Complete your 3 International distributor enrollment and review the compensation plan basics (retail profit margins, team building bonuses, rank advancement structure). Study the core product catalog—immune support formulas, multivitamins, energy supplements, digestive health products, and wellness packs. Conduct your own personal health assessment using the wellness questionnaire tools provided by corporate—identify your own nutritional gaps, energy concerns, immune support needs. Order a personal wellness pack or targeted supplements addressing your specific health goals. This personal product experience becomes your credibility foundation.
  2. Week 1: Product Experience & Ingredient Education — Spend 7 days using your 3 International supplements consistently and documenting your experience—energy levels (1-10 scale), immune resilience observations, digestive improvements, overall wellness changes. Study ingredient education materials for each product you're using—understand bioavailability factors, third-party testing processes, quality sourcing standards. Learn to read supplement facts labels professionally. Review scientific documentation for key ingredients in your products. Focus on becoming genuinely knowledgeable about what you're taking and why it matters, not just memorizing marketing claims.
  3. Week 2: Wellness Consultation Skills — Practice conducting informal wellness assessments with friends or family (not selling yet, just learning). Ask about their energy levels, immune concerns, nutritional gaps, current supplement usage, wellness goals. Learn to identify which 3 International product categories address common concerns—chronic fatigue → energy support formulas, frequent illness → immune boosters, digestive issues → gut health products, overall wellness → comprehensive vitamin packs. Study how to explain supplement benefits without making medical claims. Review compliance guidelines for health product representation. Master the difference between "supports immune function" (compliant) versus "prevents illness" (non-compliant claim).
  4. Week 3: Educational Content & First Wellness Conversations — Share nutrition education content on social media—posts about common nutritional deficiencies, bioavailability of supplements, ingredient quality factors, wellness optimization approaches. Tag these with health topics, not sales messaging. Engage with 5-10 people who respond or comment on your wellness content—offer to send them a free wellness assessment questionnaire. Conduct your first 3-5 informal wellness consultations with people who express interest. Focus entirely on understanding their health goals and educating about nutrition—don't push product sales yet. Document their wellness goals and concerns for future follow-up.
  5. Week 3-4: Sample Distribution & Results Tracking — If your enrollment package included product samples or if you can afford sample packets, distribute 5-10 trial servings to people from your wellness consultations who showed genuine interest. Create simple results tracking tools—energy level charts, symptom journals, wellness goal progress sheets. Follow up with sample recipients at Day 2, Day 5, and Day 7 to check their experience. Ask specific questions: "How's your energy compared to before?" "Any digestive improvements?" "How are you feeling overall?" Document positive responses for future testimonial use (with permission).
  6. Week 4: First Customers & Protocol Building — Convert 2-3 of your sample recipients into paying customers by creating personalized supplement protocols—specific product recommendations based on their wellness goals, clear usage instructions, realistic expectation setting about timeline for results. Explain the customer enrollment process and preferred customer benefits (wholesale pricing, autoship discounts). Set up their first orders and explain autoship options for consistency. Create follow-up schedules to track their ongoing wellness journey. Celebrate these first customer wins with your upline sponsor—get feedback on your consultation approach and protocol design.
  7. Day 30: Master the 3 International AI Playbook — Return to this page and implement each AI recruiting tactic systematically. Set up automated wellness assessment funnels, build progressive nutrition education sequences, create goal-based lane routing for different health concerns, develop ingredient transparency content, establish results tracking systems, design complementary product education, and package your entire wellness consultation system for new distributors you recruit. The AI infrastructure does the educational heavy lifting—prospects get consistent nutrition education and health credibility regardless of your personal schedule.
  8. 30 Days Forward: Business Opportunity Positioning — Once you have personal product results, customer success stories, and wellness credibility established, begin positioning the 3 International business opportunity. Share your own health transformation journey and how it led to helping others. Explain the business model: "I help people optimize their wellness through quality supplements, and I get paid on product sales plus team building." Identify which of your customers might be interested in earning income by sharing products they already love. Use your wellness consultation system as your business presentation—show prospects they can duplicate your educational approach without needing healthcare credentials.
  9. Duplicate & Scale — Package your wellness assessment tools, nutrition education content library, product protocol templates, consultation scripts, sample distribution process, results tracking systems, and follow-up sequences into a turnkey system for new team members. Train them on the same 30-day roadmap you followed—personal product experience → ingredient education → wellness consultation skills → sample distribution → customer acquisition → business opportunity positioning. The goal: every new distributor can launch with health credibility within their first week by duplicating your documented system.
  10. Ongoing: Build Wellness Thought Leadership — Continue sharing nutrition education content consistently—immune support tips during cold season, energy optimization strategies for busy professionals, supplement quality factors consumers should know, common nutritional deficiencies by age group. Position yourself as a trusted wellness resource, not just a supplement salesperson. Engage with health-conscious communities, answer questions genuinely, provide value before ever mentioning business opportunity. The credibility you build through educational content becomes your most powerful recruiting asset—prospects already trust your wellness knowledge before they ever see the 3 International compensation plan.
