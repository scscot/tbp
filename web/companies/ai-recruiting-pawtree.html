What pawTree Sells (in plain English)

Founded by Roger Morgan in 2012 and launched commercially in 2014, pawTree offers premium dog and cat food, treats, and supplements with no fillers or by-products—just nutritionally dense ingredients and high-quality antioxidants. Roger assembled a team of veterinarians and a PhD in Animal Science and Pet Nutrition to formulate products that prioritize pet health over profit margins. The company currently offers 12 dog food recipes and more than a dozen treat varieties, all distributed exclusively through independent representatives called petPros who provide one-on-one customer care. Unlike retail pet food brands sold through stores, pawTree creates personalized pet nutrition plans and builds relationships between petPros and pet parents, offering consultative service that big-box stores cannot match.

The petPro model differentiates pawTree from retail pet food—customers get personalized nutrition guidance, breed-specific recommendations, and ongoing support rather than guessing in a store aisle overwhelmed by options.

Who Buys pawTree

pawTree attracts devoted pet parents seeking premium nutrition for their dogs and cats, health-conscious pet owners who read ingredient labels and avoid fillers, performance-focused customers with working or sporting dogs, and pet lovers frustrated by generic retail options who want personalized guidance. The business opportunity appeals to animal lovers wanting to turn passion into income, pet professionals like groomers and trainers seeking complementary revenue streams, network marketers drawn to the pet industry's growth and customer loyalty, and entrepreneurs who appreciate relationship-based selling. Customers earn up to 35% commission after initial sales, with residual structures that create ongoing income from loyal pet parents.

Pet owners are incredibly loyal customers—when they find food that works for their pet, they reorder monthly for years, creating reliable residual income for petPros.

How pawTree Distributes

pawTree provides petPros with personal e-commerce storefronts, pet nutrition education, marketing materials, sample programs, and a multi-level compensation plan that rewards both retail sales and team building. The direct selling model bypasses traditional retail markups, allowing premium quality at competitive prices while compensating petPros for consultation services. Training emphasizes pet nutrition fundamentals, ingredient quality standards, breed-specific recommendations, health concern solutions, and customer relationship management. As a social selling model, pawTree focuses on building trust and delivering value rather than transactional sales, positioning petPros as pet care advisors.

Key insight: pawTree's MLM structure means each layer of petPro earns a cut, which drives retail pricing higher than some mass-market brands—positioning value requires emphasizing ingredient quality, personalized service, and pet health outcomes rather than competing on price alone.

Getting Started with pawTree: Your First 30 Days

New to pawTree? Here's your step-by-step roadmap to launching your pet nutrition petPro journey with premium ingredient positioning and personalized pet care consultation:

  1. Day 1-3: Foundation & Personal Pet Experience — Complete your pawTree petPro enrollment and order your personal pet products immediately (required for credibility—you cannot sell pet nutrition you don't personally use with your own pets). Study the compensation plan structure (retail profit margins, customer referral bonuses, team building commissions, rank advancement milestones). Purchase products your own dog or cat will use daily—select food formulas appropriate for their breed size, life stage, and any health concerns, plus treats and supplements if relevant. Begin feeding your pet pawTree from Day 1, documenting their experience meticulously—take before photos of coat condition, note energy levels throughout the day, track digestion quality, monitor any skin or allergy improvements. This personal pet transformation becomes your most authentic selling tool, so detailed observation is critical from the very beginning.
  2. Week 1: Pet Nutrition Education & Ingredient Quality — Spend 7 days studying pawTree's nutritional philosophy and ingredient standards to build consultation confidence. Study the no-filler formulation approach—understand why pawTree excludes corn, wheat, soy, and by-products, and how this impacts pet health compared to mass-market brands with 40-50% filler content. Learn the antioxidant benefit system—memorize how vitamins E and C, selenium, and other antioxidants support immune function, coat health, and longevity. Study protein quality standards—understand the difference between named meat sources (chicken, beef, salmon) versus generic "meat meal" or "animal fat" in competitor products. Review the veterinary development story—how Roger Morgan's team of veterinarians and PhD nutritionists formulated recipes prioritizing pet health over profit margins. Practice explaining these concepts in simple language pet parents understand without overwhelming them with technical jargon—your goal is confident, credible consultation that positions pawTree as premium quality worth the investment.
  3. Week 1: Health Concern Consultation Framework — Develop your pet health consultation approach by studying common issues and pawTree solutions without making veterinary medical claims. Learn to identify weight management needs—how to recommend appropriate formulas and portion guidance for overweight pets while encouraging veterinary partnership for medical obesity. Study sensitive stomach solutions—understand which recipes and ingredients help dogs with digestive issues, and how to guide feeding transitions gradually to avoid upset. Learn joint health support—memorize supplement options for senior pets or active dogs with mobility concerns, positioning as nutritional support rather than medical treatment. Study skin and coat improvement pathways—understand how omega fatty acids, protein quality, and antioxidants visibly improve coat shine, skin health, and allergy symptoms over 4-8 weeks. Practice the consultation language that provides helpful guidance while respecting veterinary boundaries—"Many pet parents see improvements in..." rather than "This will cure..."—so you build trust without liability concerns.
  4. Week 2: Pet Parent Prospect List & Targeting Strategy — Create your initial prospect list of 20-30 pet owners you personally know, organized by health concern priority for strategic outreach. List pet parents with visible pet health challenges—overweight dogs, pets with skin allergies, low-energy senior animals, picky eaters, dogs with dull coats. Include active pet owners who invest in their pets—people who already buy premium products, visit groomers regularly, or participate in dog sports and activities. Add pet professionals in your network—groomers, trainers, dog walkers, pet sitters who interact with multiple pet parents and could benefit from product recommendations or business opportunity. Include friends who complain about pet food confusion—overwhelmed pet parents frustrated by retail store options who would appreciate personalized guidance. Organize your list by warmth (close friends first, acquaintances later) and by health concern (so you can target your initial outreach with relevant transformation stories). This strategic organization ensures your first consultations have the highest conversion probability.
  5. Week 2-3: Personal Pet Transformation Documentation — Continue feeding your own pet pawTree while documenting observable changes for authentic storytelling in consultations. Take weekly photos of your pet—coat shine improvement, eye brightness, energy during walks, body condition changes. Track measurable metrics—stool quality consistency, eating enthusiasm, scratching reduction if allergies were present, mobility improvement if joint issues existed. Document any unexpected improvements—many pet parents notice benefits they didn't anticipate, like reduced shedding, better breath, or calmer behavior. Create simple before-after comparison content—side-by-side photos with brief captions explaining what changed and timeline. Practice telling your pet's transformation story in 60 seconds—concise, authentic narrative that demonstrates why you personally believe in pawTree quality. This personal experience becomes your most powerful recruiting tool because it's genuine, specific, and demonstrates you use what you sell.
  6. Week 3: Initial Pet Consultations & Sample Strategy — Conduct 10-15 pet parent consultations using a simple assessment framework to recommend products and offer sample experiences. Reach out to your warmest prospects with a consultation offer—"I'm learning pet nutrition through pawTree and offering free personalized assessments—can I ask about your dog/cat and recommend some options?" Conduct brief consultations asking breed, age, weight, activity level, current food, and any health concerns. Recommend specific pawTree products based on their pet's needs—explain why you're suggesting particular formulas, emphasizing ingredient quality and health benefits relevant to their situation. Offer product samples when appropriate—pawTree provides sample programs that let pet parents trial products before committing to full-size bags, reducing purchase resistance. Schedule follow-up conversations 7-10 days after sample delivery to discuss pet response, answer questions, and guide toward purchase. Track consultation outcomes meticulously—who showed interest, who ordered samples, who had concerns, who seemed entrepreneurial—so you can prioritize follow-up strategically.
  7. Week 3-4: First Customer Enrollments & Conversion — Convert 2-3 sample recipients or consultation prospects into paying customers by addressing objections and emphasizing value proposition. Follow up with sample users asking specific questions—"How did your dog respond to the food? Did you notice any coat changes or energy improvements?" Address price objections by calculating cost-per-feeding compared to premium retail brands, emphasizing ingredient density means less food waste and better nutrition per dollar. Offer starter bundles or first-order promotions if available through pawTree to reduce initial purchase hesitation. Guide feeding transition protocols—explain how to gradually mix pawTree with current food over 7-10 days to avoid digestive upset, positioning this as professional guidance. Secure first orders through your personal petPro storefront, ensuring customers understand monthly auto-ship options for convenience and ensuring you earn residual commissions. Celebrate each customer enrollment—send thank-you messages, check in after their first shipment arrives, continue positioning yourself as their ongoing pet nutrition resource.
  8. Week 4: Business Opportunity Introduction & Recruiting — Identify entrepreneurial prospects from your initial consultations and introduce the petPro business opportunity to 3-5 candidates. Watch for entrepreneurial signals during customer conversations—pet parents who ask lots of questions about products, express excitement about results, mention they tell friends, or comment "I should be selling this!" Approach warm prospects with business curiosity—"You're so passionate about pet nutrition—have you thought about the petPro side? There's a business opportunity with customers who reorder monthly." Share the income overview—explain retail profit margins (up to 35% after qualification), residual customer income from loyal pet parents, and team building potential. Provide enrollment information and compensation plan summary, emphasizing low startup investment and flexibility to work part-time around other commitments. Offer to sponsor them personally if they're interested—explain your support, training access, and how they'd plug into your team system. Even if no one enrolls in Week 4, planting business opportunity seeds positions you as team builder and creates future recruiting pipeline.
  9. Day 30: Master AI Playbook & Duplication Systems — Implement the AI recruiting tactics from this guide to systematize pet consultations, automate education, and create duplication for future team members. Build or adopt pet profile automation tools—interactive assessments that collect breed, age, weight, and health concerns, then generate personalized product recommendations and follow-up sequences. Create ingredient education libraries—content explaining no-filler formulations, antioxidant benefits, protein quality, and veterinary development that you can share with prospects at appropriate consultation stages. Develop health concern lane routing—segment prospects into weight management, sensitive stomach, joint health, skin and coat, or general wellness lanes, each receiving customized content and transformation stories. Organize pet transformation galleries—permission-based before-after success stories from customers, organized by health concern and breed so you recommend the most relevant example to each prospect. Package your consultation process into a duplicatable system—assessment questionnaires, feeding calculators, transition protocols, troubleshooting guides—so when you recruit petPros, they become confident consultants in one week without needing veterinary expertise.
  10. Ongoing: Build Pet Parent Community & Positioning — Establish yourself as a trusted pet wellness resource through consistent education, transformation sharing, and authentic relationship building that naturally generates customer and recruiting referrals. Create or join online pet parent communities—Facebook groups, email lists, or social media presences where you share daily pet tips, nutrition education, seasonal health warnings, and customer transformation spotlights. Share educational content consistently—ingredient guides, feeding tips, health concern solutions, product highlights—that provide value whether people buy from you or not, building trust and authority. Feature customer transformations regularly—with permission, share before-after stories highlighting visible improvements in coat quality, energy, health issues resolution, reinforcing pawTree's effectiveness. Soft-introduce business opportunity periodically—"Many pet parents ask me about the petPro side—if you're curious about turning pet passion into income, let me know" without pressuring. Continue feeding your own pet pawTree and documenting ongoing results—your personal long-term experience remains your most authentic marketing. Position as pet wellness partner, not salesperson—your goal is helping pets thrive through better nutrition, and pawTree happens to be your solution, creating natural referrals from satisfied pet parents.
