What SendOutCards Sells (in plain English)

SendOutCards is a relationship marketing platform founded in 2003 that enables individuals and businesses to send personalized physical greeting cards, gifts, and promotional materials through a mobile app or web interface. The core offering is custom greeting cards with handwriting simulation technology that makes digital text appear as authentic handwritten messages—cards cost under $1 each including postage and are mailed directly to recipients without distributors handling physical inventory. The platform features birthday reminders, automated card campaigns, bulk sending for businesses, photo cards, custom artwork uploads, and integration with contact management systems. SendOutCards also offers personalized gifts including brownies, cookies, chocolates, teddy bears, flowers, candles, and custom gift boxes that pair with cards. The business model centers on relationship marketing automation—sending appreciation cards to clients, prospects, and team members systematically rather than sporadically. Distributors use the system personally while building teams of others who value consistent relationship cultivation through physical card-sending.

SendOutCards fills the "relationship touchpoint automation" niche—in a digital world where text messages get ignored and emails go to spam, physical cards with handwritten-style messages stand out and create emotional connections that generate referrals and repeat business.

Who Buys SendOutCards

SendOutCards attracts relationship-focused professionals including real estate agents (client appreciation systems), insurance agents (policy anniversary cards), financial advisors (birthday and milestone recognition), small business owners (customer retention campaigns), network marketers (team recognition and prospect follow-up), wedding planners and event professionals (vendor appreciation), authors and speakers (audience engagement), coaches and consultants (client touchpoints), and individuals who value personal connections over digital-only communication. The customer base spans ages 35-65 with emphasis on entrepreneurs and sales professionals who understand referral-based business growth. The business opportunity appeals to people who already send cards manually and recognize automation value, former greeting card hobbyists seeking monetization, relationship-oriented networkers tired of product inventory, international entrepreneurs (platform operates globally), and individuals attracted by 100M+ cards delivered track record and residual income from customer subscriptions.

Strong appeal to "relationship investors"—people who believe handwritten cards create business advantage but lack time for manual writing, addressing, and mailing processes.

How SendOutCards Distributes

SendOutCards operates a compensation plan offering commissions on card purchases (both personal and customer accounts), team building bonuses from distributor enrollment, residual income from ongoing customer subscriptions, and rank advancement incentives. Distributors earn through direct customer acquisition (businesses and individuals who pay monthly subscription fees plus per-card costs), personal card usage demonstrating the platform, and recruiting other distributors who build their own customer bases. The system emphasizes no physical inventory—cards are printed and mailed from SendOutCards facilities after digital submission. Rank progression includes various leadership levels with increasing team bonuses and global income opportunities. Distributors can focus purely on personal card-sending with small customer bases or scale through large teams teaching relationship marketing systems to thousands of business professionals.

Key insight: No physical inventory eliminates storage, shipping, and upfront product costs—distributors sell digital card-sending capability and relationship marketing systems rather than physical goods.

Training & Onboarding Snapshot

SendOutCards emphasizes relationship marketing philosophy, platform navigation, and heartfelt card-sending strategies. New distributors learn about the mobile app interface, handwriting font selection, photo upload techniques, gift selection and pairing, campaign automation for birthdays and anniversaries, contact importing from CRM systems, and tracking delivered cards through online training modules, live webinars, and sponsor mentorship. The company provides card template libraries, occasion-based messaging guides, and business card campaign examples. However, traditional training focuses on personal card-sending testimonials and manual relationship building—leaving systematic digital prospecting, automated follow-up sequences beyond the SendOutCards platform, social media content strategies for attracting relationship-focused professionals, and scalable customer acquisition largely unaddressed. Most distributors struggle translating "I send cards and love it" passion into consistent prospecting conversations beyond immediate warm market circles who already know them.
