What Amway Sells (in plain English)

Founded in 1959 in Ada, Michigan by Jay Van Andel and Rich DeVos, Amway is one of the world's largest direct selling companies with 65 years of history. The company's flagship brand is Nutrilite, the largest-selling vitamin and supplement brand globally, which Amway has owned since 1994. Beyond Nutrilite, Amway offers Artistry skincare and cosmetics, XS Energy sports nutrition drinks, home care products, and 350+ other wellness and lifestyle items. Operating in over 100 countries with billions in annual revenue, Amway was named among America's top 100 largest private companies by Forbes in 2024.

The business opportunity angle is central to Amway's identity—this isn't just about selling products, it's about building legacy income and generational wealth through entrepreneurship.

Who Buys Amway

Amway customers span wellness enthusiasts seeking premium Nutrilite supplements, beauty-conscious consumers attracted to Artistry's prestige positioning, athletes using XS Energy for performance, and eco-conscious buyers choosing concentrated home care products. The business opportunity attracts aspiring entrepreneurs across demographics—from young professionals seeking side income to retirees building legacy businesses. The global footprint means Amway IBOs serve diverse cultural markets from North America to Asia-Pacific.

Personal storefronts make it easy for customers to reorder—the challenge is getting them to the storefront the first time.

How Amway Distributes

Amway provides IBOs with personal online storefronts, supply chain infrastructure, and a compensation plan that pays commissions on product sales plus bonuses for team building. Crucially, Amway IBOs earn no commissions for simply recruiting—income is tied to actual product movement. The plan includes retail profit margins, performance bonuses, and leadership incentives tied to organizational volume. Amway has over 151,000 life insurance licensed representatives in the U.S. alone as of December 2024.

Key insight: Amway's longevity and infrastructure mean duplication works—if you can systematize your recruiting and follow-up process.

Training & Onboarding Snapshot

Amway emphasizes the business opportunity first, product education second. IBOs are trained on personal development, goal setting, business presentations, and leveraging Amway's supply chain and storefront tools. Corporate resources include online learning platforms, regional events, and annual international conferences. The challenge: traditional Amway training relies heavily on in-person meetings and one-on-one presentations—digital follow-up and automated nurture sequences are often neglected.
