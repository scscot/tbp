What eXp Realty Offers (in plain English)

eXp Realty is a cloud-based real estate brokerage operating entirely in a virtual environment without physical office locations. Agents access training, collaboration tools, and brokerage services through eXp World, a 3D virtual campus built in a gaming-style platform. The core offering includes competitive commission splits (80/20 with cap), stock awards (EXPI shares on NASDAQ), revenue-sharing opportunities from agent sponsorship, and comprehensive training through eXp University.

Unlike traditional MLM companies, eXp Realty is a licensed real estate brokerage where agents earn primarily from real estate transactions. The revenue-share component rewards agents for attracting other licensed agents to the brokerage—similar to a team override structure but grounded in real estate production. Additional offerings include kvCORE technology platform, lead generation tools, mentorship programs, and global expansion opportunities across 24+ countries.

Key insight: eXp attracts agents seeking lower overhead, equity ownership, and passive income from agent attraction—not product sales.

Markets & Footprint

eXp Realty operates in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, South Africa, India, Mexico, Portugal, France, Puerto Rico, Brazil, Italy, Hong Kong, Colombia, Spain, Israel, Panama, Germany, Dominican Republic, Greece, New Zealand, Chile, Poland, and Dubai. The cloud-based model enables rapid international expansion without physical office infrastructure.

International reach means your agent attraction must work across real estate licensing systems and commission structures.

Distribution Model

eXp Realty uses an 80/20 commission split until agents reach a $16,000 cap annually, then 100% commission. Agents can earn revenue share (up to 5% of gross commission income) from sponsored agents across 7 tiers. Stock awards include EXPI shares for production milestones and agent attraction. Icon Agent status (20+ agent sponsors) unlocks additional equity and bonuses. Agents must maintain active real estate licenses.

Key insight: Lead with education and brokerage value. Revenue-share is a bonus, not the primary pitch.

Training & Onboarding Snapshot

eXp Realty emphasizes agent education through eXp University (live classes, certifications, designation courses), eXp World campus tours, ICON Agent training, and technology onboarding for kvCORE and cloud collaboration tools. New agents receive sponsor mentorship, state-specific compliance training, and business planning workshops. The company hosts annual EXPCON conferences and regional meetups. The challenge: conveying the cloud-based model and revenue-share opportunity without appearing like an MLM recruitment scheme.

Your AI Recruiting Playbook for eXp Realty

  1. Lead with agent pain points. Segment prospects into high-split seekers, tech-hungry agents, passive-income builders, equity-focused investors, or team leaders needing better splits. Your message changes by agent profile.
  2. Brokerage comparison calculator. Use AI to generate personalized commission split comparisons showing net income at eXp vs. their current brokerage, including cap benefits and stock value.
  3. eXp World demo walkthrough. Create repeatable virtual campus tours with AI-generated invites, reminder sequences, and post-tour follow-up questions about pain points and goals.
  4. Education-first content series. Host "Real Estate Business Building" workshops (lead gen, systems, scaling) with eXp as the backend solution—not the primary topic. AI automates invites and follow-ups.
  5. Icon Agent roadmap builder. For agents interested in attraction, create a 90-day roadmap to Icon Agent status with weekly AI check-ins on progress and coaching tips.
  6. Objection scripts for skeptics. Pre-script responses to "is this MLM?" and "why leave my brokerage?" with brokerage license proof, NASDAQ stock charts, and licensed agent testimonials.
  7. Duplicate the education system. Save your comparison calculators, demo scripts, and workshop outlines so new agents can attract others using value—not hype.
